Section 1 · Fast Speech & Reductions · Strategy
The R.E.A.D. Method: Your Freeze Breaker
When you freeze, use R.E.A.D. Click each step to reveal what to do.
R
RELAX
Take a breath. You don't need every word to understand the message.
E
EXPECT reductions
You know they're coming now — reduced speech is normal, not careless.
A
ANCHOR on key words
Focus on nouns and verbs. They carry the meaning even in fast speech.
D
DON'T PANIC
"Could you say that again, please?" — This is professional, not weak.

Section 2 · Idioms & Adult Slang · Concept
What Is Register? Knowing When to Use What
Register = the level of formality in your language. Think of it like clothing — you don't wear the same thing to a job interview and a barbecue.
FORMAL
Work emails
Meetings
Doctor's office
Job interviews
CASUAL
Neighbors
Lunch break
Coworkers you know
Stores & errands
INFORMAL
Close friends
Family
Text messages
Social media
Rule: Adults adjust register based on who they're talking to. The same person can shift between all three in one day. You should too.

Section 3 · Phone Conversations · Introduction
Why Phone Calls Feel Impossible
No body language. No facial expressions. No second chances to ask "what?"

Phone calls are the hardest real-world English challenge for most advanced learners. You lose every visual cue you normally rely on — and natives don't slow down.

In this section, you'll learn the phrases, the strategies, and the confidence to handle any call.

Section 3 · Phone Conversations · Strategy
When You Miss Something: Graceful Recovery
Missing something doesn't end the call. How you recover does. Use these three moves — in order if needed.
1
CLARIFY
"I'm sorry, could you say that last part again?"
Simple, polite, works every time.
2
CONFIRM
"So you're saying [repeat what you heard] — is that right?"
Shows you were listening and gives them a chance to correct you.
3
BRIDGE
"Let me make sure I have this correctly..." — buys you time to think without going silent.
Important: Asking for clarification is professionalism, not weakness.
